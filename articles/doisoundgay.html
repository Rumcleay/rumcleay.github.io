YOU'VE GOT A GAY VOICE



'DO I SOUND GAY?' A DOCUMENTARY BY DAVID THROPE

This documentary came out in 2014, but I didn't have a website then. It follows a man who is pretty upset about his voice and how gay it sounds, even though he's surrounded by men with arguably 'gayer' voices. This setting alone demonstrates the direction this film is headed in its exploration of the voice. Whilst it does argue that the gravity of 'gay' attached to one's voice correlates to the internalised homophobia of a person, it also explores the biologically determined factors that contribute to the phenomenon of 'sounding gay', including the link between homosexuality and lisps.

Personally, I find the voice one of the most erotic parts of a person, and am therefore quite unusually interested in this film. Not that I'm sat there working out who I'm falling in and out of love with, but clearly the fear of sounding uninteresting, uncomfortable etc. that comes with speech must contribute even more to the black hole of anxiety that springs from the stigma attached to owning a 'gay voice'.
